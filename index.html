About

I am a PhD student working on the discovery and development of new scintilllator materials in the Scintillation Materials Reserach Center at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. My specific area of study is multi-componet garnet ceramics.

My favorite part of my work is the sense of accomplishment and true understanding that comes from working genuinely hard on something. I'm amazed by the complexity of the world I get to live in and I want to learn a little more about it every day.

I have a master's degree in Materials Science and Engineering from the University of Tennessee and a Bachelor's of Science from Missouri University of Science and Technology. My PhD advisor is Dr. Mariya Zhuravleva, and I was an undergraduate research assistant for Dr. Gregory Hilmas at Missouri University of Science and Technology and Dr. Ambreen Nisar at Florida International University.
